Our Admission

Our high school's admissions process is designed to welcome diverse and motivated students into our close-knit educational community. We value academic excellence, creativity, and a commitment to personal growth. Our dedicated admissions team carefully considers each applicant, ensuring a fair and holistic evaluation of their potential. We embrace inclusivity and encourage students from all backgrounds to apply. Beyond academic achievements, we recognize the importance of character, leadership, and community involvement. Our aim is to foster an environment where students thrive intellectually , socially, and emotionally. We invite students to embark on a journey of exploration, knowledge, and self-discovery, contributing to a vibrant and inclusive high school experience.

Curriculum

Civic

Civics is the study of an individual's role in society and their relationship with the government. It encompasses understanding civic rights, responsibilities, and participation in the democratic process. Civics educates citizens about the functioning of government institutions, laws, and the importance of active engagement in shaping a just and equitable society.

Amharic

Amharic is the official language of Ethiopia, spoken by millions as their native tongue. It belongs to the Semitic language family, characterized by a unique script and rich linguistic history. Amharic serves as a unifying force in Ethiopia, reflecting the nation's diverse cultures and heritage.

English

English is a globally spoken language, with over a billion speakers worldwide. It's renowned for its versatility and serves as a lingua franca in various fields, including business, science, and entertainment. English's rich history and influence make it a vital tool for communication and cultural exchange on a global scale.

Mathematics

Mathematics, often referred to as math, is a fundamental field of study that explores the relationships between numbers, shapes, and patterns. It provides a universal language for understanding and solving real-world problems, from basic arithmetic to advanced calculus. Math plays a crucial role in science, technology, engineering, and various aspects of daily life.

Physics

Physics is the fundamental science that explores the fundamental laws and principles governing the universe. It seeks to understand the behavior of matter, energy, space, and time. From subatomic particles to celestial bodies, physics delves into the forces, motion, and interactions that shape our physical reality, driving technological advancements and scientific breakthroughs.

Chemistry

Chemistry is the scientific study of matter, its properties, composition, structure, and the changes it undergoes during chemical reactions. It explores the fundamental building blocks of the universe, from atoms and molecules to complex substances. Chemistry plays a vital role in countless industries, from medicine to energy production, shaping our world.

Biology

Biology is the scientific study of living organisms and their interactions with each other and their environment. It explores the complexities of life, from the smallest cells to entire ecosystems. Through observation, experimentation, and analysis, biology seeks to understand the mechanisms of life processes, genetics, evolution, and ecological systems.

Afaan Oromoo

Afaan Oromoo, also known as Oromo, is a Cushitic language spoken by the Oromo people in Ethiopia and parts of Kenya. With millions of speakers, it's one of the world's largest Afroasiatic languages. Afaan Oromoo plays a vital role in Oromo culture, identity, and communication, fostering community cohesion.

Geography

Geography is the study of the Earth's physical features, climate, human populations , and their interactions. It explores the spatial relationships between places, examining phenomena such as landscapes, ecosystems, cultures, and economies. Geography helps us understand how our world is shaped and how we, as humans, impact it.

History

History is the study of past events, people, societies, and their impact on the present. It explores the evolution of civilizations, cultures, and ideas, providing insights into human achievements and mistakes. By examining the past, history offers valuable lessons and perspectives, helping us understand the complexities of our world.

Economics

Economics is the social science that examines the production, distribution, and consumption of goods and services. It explores how individuals, businesses, and governments make choices to allocate limited resources. Economics helps us understand pricing, market behavior, and the impact of policies on wealth, employment, and societal well-being.

General Business

General business refers to the broad field encompassing various commercial activities, from entrepreneurship to management, marketing, finance, and operations. It involves the creation, operation, and growth of organizations, focusing on profit generation and sustainable practices. General business skills are essential for navigating the complexities of the corporate world.

Technical Drawing

Technical drawing, also known as drafting, is a precise and structured form of illustration used in engineering, architecture, and manufacturing. It communicates intricate details of objects, machinery, and structures through standardized symbols and dimensions. These drawings serve as blueprints, aiding in the accurate construction and understanding of complex designs.

Information Technology (IT)

Information Technology (IT) encompasses the use of computers, networks, software, and digital systems to manage and process data. It plays a fundamental role in modern society, supporting businesses, communication, and innovation. IT professionals develop, maintain, and secure technology infrastructure, enabling the digital transformation of various industries worldwide.

Clubs

Civic

Civic clubs in schools are student organizations dedicated to community engagement and service. They foster social responsibility, leadership, and teamwork among students. These clubs undertake various philanthropic initiatives, volunteer work, and civic projects to make a positive impact within and beyond their school communities, nurturing future civic-minded leaders.

Health

Health clubs in schools are extracurricular groups or programs dedicated to promoting physical well-being and healthy lifestyles among students. They often offer fitness activities, nutritional education, and wellness initiatives. These clubs aim to instill lifelong habits of exercise and nutrition, contributing to students' overall health and well-being.

Mini Media

Mini media clubs in schools are student organizations focused on media production and storytelling. These clubs empower students to explore photography, filmmaking, journalism, and social media management. Through creative projects and workshops, students develop essential communication skills, media literacy, and a platform to express their ideas and perspectives.

Events

School events encompass a wide range of activities, from educational seminars and parent-teacher conferences to sports competitions and cultural festivals. These events foster community engagement, encourage student participation, and provide opportunities for learning and social interaction, enhancing the overall educational experience within the school environment.
